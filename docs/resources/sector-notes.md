Sector notes capture sector-specific interpretation without making sectors the organizing principle of the site. Start from the same reader problems and evidence needs, then add sector context where it changes sufficiency, vocabulary, retention, or assurance expectations.

## Sector Note Template

| Field | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Sector | Which sector or domain is being considered? |
| Driver | What regulation, procurement expectation, customer assurance demand, audit, certification, or risk driver matters? |
| Decision | What decision is the reader trying to make? |
| Evidence | Which artifacts matter most in this sector? |
| Lifecycle emphasis | Which lifecycle stages are especially important? |
| Standards context | Which standards or frameworks create demand, define evidence expectations, or support implementation? |
| Limits | What should not be implied? |

## Candidate Sectors

- Critical infrastructure.
- Public sector and defense.
- Healthcare and medical devices.
- Cloud infrastructure components.
- Mobile and PC platforms.
- Industrial and operational technology.
- Automotive platforms.
- Enterprise buyers.

## Guardrail

Do not create sector-specific pages unless there is enough real content to help the reader make decisions. Otherwise, keep sector notes short and link back to the general guidance, evidence, and standards pages.
